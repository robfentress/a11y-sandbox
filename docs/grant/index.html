Accessibility Professional Certification Grant

About the grant

Accessible Technologies, will be awarding grants to selected candidates throughout the university to become certified accessibility professionals.  In order to have the most impact on improving the accessibility of the university's web sites and web aplications, we are looking to distribute these grants to individuals in strategic positions throughout the university who are able to effectively serve as change agents. 

The benefits and expectations for those who participate are as follows:

Benefits

  • Technology-Enhanced Learning and Online Strategies (TLOS) will pay for:
    • Membership in the International Association for Accessibility Professionals (IAAP), through August 2019, a $175 value, with options to renew based on continued participation in Web Developer Subcommittee of the Accessibility Network.
    • Online asynchronous IAAP Certified Professional in Accessibility Core Competencies (CPACC) Certification Preparation Course from Deque University (5 hours worth of material), a $45 value
    • Online asynchronous IAAP Web Accessibility Specialist (WAS) Exam Preparation from Deque University (20 hours worth of material), a $150 value
    • All fees required to take the CPACC and WAS certification exams, $325 and $375 values respectively.
  • Accessible Technologies will:
    • Provide access to assistive technologies and expert guidance on their use
    • Answer questions regarding exam-prep course materials
    • Provide physical and virtual space for regular study-group meetings for participants as they progress through exam prep courses

Expectations

  • Devote 2 hours a week over the course of 10 weeks to the completion of exam prep course materials
  • Meet for an hour once weekly for 10 weeks as a group with other participants as they progress together through the exam prep course materials.
  • Take the certification exams at a registered testing center (likely the Roanoke Higher Education Center)
  • After passing the certification exams, commit to serving a one-year term on the Web Developer Subcommittee of the Accessibility Network.  This committee will meet once every other week for at least one hour. Goals for this subcommittee for 2018 will include:
    • Collaborating to develop a reliable and realistic process for:
      • Testing individual web pages for accessibility
      • Auditing websites and web applications for accessibility (including sampling methodologies and report formats)
    • In order to validate the agreed-upon process for auditing websites or web applications, all participants will review a common website or web application to be agreed upon by the subcommittee.
    • Identify the top five websites or web applications whose remediation will be most successful in mitigating risk by enhancing access.
    • Assist in the selection of enterprise web accessibility testing software.
    • Help devise strategies for increasing participation in the Web Developer Subcommittee
  • For a website or web application in your circle of influence,
    • Conduct an initial review of that website or web application, using the process developed previously by the Web Developer Subcommittee.
    • Fix any problems revealed by that review.
    • Conduct a final review of that website or web application, using the process developed previously by the Web Developer Subcommittee.
  • Within your circle of influence, serve as a channel for dissemination of information regarding web accessibility best practice, and a conduit for gathering information about needs for training, assistance, and tools.

Application

All questions must be answered.

Is the person you report to aware of the expectations detailed above and does she or he support your participation as described?
